Portents

A Class X Portent, believed to herald the waking of the Great Golden Beast.

Under the You Shall Know Them By The Weeping Of The Innocent Act of 1979, all citizens 7 or older are subject to mandatory portent reporting requirements. To avoid significant fines, make sure you know what the signs are and who to report them to!

Making frivolous portent reports is a federal crime punishable with up to 5 years in prison.

Class I Portents

These signs may portend disquiet, and should be reported to the police. Failure to report a Class I Portent may result in a $10 fine.

  • A surprise visit from a family member you haven't seen for a very long time.
  • Malfunctioning appliances.
  • Disobedient children.
  • Minor heresies.
  • Darkening skies.
  • Unexpected pangs of longing.
  • Missing pens.

Remember that death, no matter how agonizing, does not exempt citizens from portent reporting requirements. (Exceptions will be made on a case-by-case basis for citizens enrolled in the Supplementary Security Income program who are judged incapable of fulfulling reporting requirements. See the Social Security website for details on how to apply for an "in case of death" portent reporting exemption.)
