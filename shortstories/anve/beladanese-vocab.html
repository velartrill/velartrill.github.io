Speaking Beladanese: Vocabulary

by Lexi Summer Hale (@velartrill)

This is a list of Beladanese words. To learn how to put them together to form meaningful sentences, see Speaking Beladanese: Chapter I and following chapters.

Key: "n" means a noun, "v" means a verb, and "d" means a descriptive verb.

A

  • ar, n: mother
  • arbara, n: "bastard" (motherless)
  • arbara, d: motherless
  • arche, d: maternal
  • arga, v: to give birth

B

  • barga, n: ass
  • bargatinga, n: asshole
  • bosta, v: own
  • bostam, v: owner
  • bostagan, n: slave

C

  • cambe, n: tree
  • cambeche, n: arboreal
  • cambedin, n: grass
  • cambescan, n: forest
  • carda, v: do
  • cardast, n: fashion
  • cardastache, d: fashionable
  • cardastagan, n: fashionista
  • corde, d: dead
  • cordeche, d: mortal
  • cordechem, n: mortal
  • cordencue, n: death, mortality
  • corma, n: unmarried man
  • cuenda, n: glass
  • cuore, v: expect

F

  • fal, n: daughter
  • falbara, d: childless
  • faltig, n: tomboy
  • fena, n: sister

P

  • parna, v: fuck
  • parna, n: sex
  • parnagan, n: sex worker
  • penda, v: know
  • puen, n: fire
  • puenche, d: on fire
  • puenhana, n: firecraft
  • puengan, n: firecrafter

G

  • gabra, n: goat
  • gabragan, n: goatherd

H

  • halla, n: river
  • hacara, v/n: rule
  • hacarabara, d: anarchic
  • hacarabaragan, n: anarchist
  • hacarabarancue, n: anarchy
  • hacaram, v: queen, mistress
  • hacarast, n: kingdom
  • hacaragan, n: subject (of a ruler)
  • hana, v: create
  • hanast, n: art, creation
  • hanagan, n: artist, scientist
  • hanagan puen, n: physicist

R

  • ramma, v: be inside of

T

  • tama, n: brother
  • tig, n: son
  • tigal, n: father, married man
  • tigalche, d: paternal
  • tigalga, v: sire
  • tinga, n: mouth
