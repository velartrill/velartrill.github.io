Rescue

from a serial by Lexi Summer Hale

“Is there anybody in this fic the main character doesn’t have sex with at some point?” asked Maranqura Derosdeshai Sefannai, scanning the text and raising a bemused eyebrow.

The pilot’s avatar shook ser head. “I don’t think so — wait, maybe Qlesextius?”

“Virchsex. Last chapter.”

“Ohhh, right.”

“Which is dumb.” Maranqura rolled her eyes. “The Empire hasn’t got anything like that level of virch tech; did the writer even bother thinking about anything that was going on?”

“Se couldn’t be bothered to figure out that the Imperial language doesn’t have the ‘quh’ sound, so probably not.”

“Riiight,” Maraqura snapped her fingers. “I wondered why these names sounded so off. —Oh good grief are you serious—“

“Oh, you just got to the dinner party, didn’t you?”

“I did, what the hell ass balls—“

“Pretty sure Imperials don’t even have the right body parts for that,” commented the pilot.

“I’ve seen enough.” Maranqura closed the manuscript with a violent gesture, the text flickering away with some extra visual effects for emphasis. “It’s going on the list.”

“So that’s ten, yes?”

“Think so. Hells, Sumath, I swear they get worse every cycle.”

“Anurdosa is going to love this one; se always has fun playing aristocrats.”

“Did Qlesextius even get a gender? I didn’t see it mentioned.”

Sumathaqri cocked ser head. “Thal’, you know, I don’t even think so.”

“‘Ooh, look at me, I’m gonna write a fic where Sharakir gets tangled up in Imperial politics and I’m not even gonna do enough research to figure out that Imperial characters have to have a fuckin’ gender!’” said Maranqura.

“Poor Sharakir gets ser ass dragged all over the galaxy,” said Sumathaqri. “It’s like, the fans want to see sim doing everything—”

“—and most of them can’t write the character worth a damn, so se loses every quality that makes sim entertaining,” finished Maranqura.

“Yeah.” Sumathaqri grinned. “Thal’, I can only imagine the sort of havoc this Hashruikir person could have wrecked on the narrative if se’d gotten ser hands on an Imperial dictionary.”

“Eww, I did not need that image.” Maranqura pantomimed throwing up. “Not one of us would make it through a single page without dying of laughing.”

“Except maybe Suan,” said Sumathaqri. “That cagey shit managed to make it through three whole paragraphs of The Ashen Bones of Distant Glory without even chuckli— well, somebody wants to get a hold of you in a hurry.”

A gleaming indicator had blinked into existence between them. “Shit,” said Maranqura, “we’re still off grid, aren’t we?”

“Yeah,” said Sumathaqri. “Must have prepped a buoy specially. Should I let you take this?”

Maranqura nodded. “Yeah. Sorry.”

“No problem; let me know when you’re done.” Sumathaqri’s avatar winked out of existence.

Maranqura summoned the comms screen. A manifest spilled out in front of her, crammed with logs; it looked like part of a case file. She glanced at the origin. It was from the Transit Task Force, with an unfamiliar name — “Shishara”. She opened the main message.

Hi, Maranqura. Sorry to dump more on you at the last minute, but a ship, the Forward Edge of Infinity, just went dark in your region and you’re the closest forces in the area. Will you be able to take a look?

“No problem, Shishara,” Maranqura sent back. “The mothership needs to keep orbit around Kurasan, but I’ll send out a skiff or two.”

“Thanks,” came the reply half a riaqa later. “It shouldn’t be a big deal.”

Maranqura scanned the archive briefly to make sure nothing was classified, then pinged Sumathaqri. Ser avatar reappeared. “What’s up?”

“Ship’s gone dark about ten qiyal rimwards of us,” said Maranqura. “Transit wants us to take a look.” She linked Sumathaqri in to the message so se could see it on ser end. “Can we ’port out a few skiffs?”

“Sure thing,” said Sumathaqri, scanning the manifest. “Are we expecting resistance?”

“It could be. Ships don’t go dark very often. But I mean, it could just be a prank, for all we know.”

“Should I pre-comp a return jump just in case?” said Sumathaqri. “I mean I doubt there’s a Society invasion fleet on the other end but I’d like to send our people in with an escape route ready.”

Maranqura hesitated, and ran the math. 11.6 qiyal by the volume of a skiff would take about 13 riaqa if they put most of the onboard processor time to the task; so double that for the return trip—

“I guess another 13 ri’ probably isn’t going to make a huge difference.” She glanced at the ship’s nav. “Do we jump first or do the skiffs? We still have one left to go before we’re in the Kurasan system.”

“We might as well, we’ve got most of the jump calc done for the whole ship, so it’ll save us some time. Actually, I think — yeah, it’ll shave some time off the return calculations too. So we can have the skiffs out in 24 riaqa instead of 26.”

“Okay, sounds good. …Hopefully nobody dies in those extra 11 riaqa.”

“Your call, Maran.”

“I know, I know. I mean, probably either they’re dead already or they’ll keep for a bit longer, so it’s probably safe.” She glanced at the nav. Four riaqa to jump. “These past few mroqa have just been so encouraging, haven’t they.”

“You’re telling me. I know somebody on Kurasan.”

“Shit!” Maranqura started. “You never told me. You know them as a…?”

“Mparisea,” said Sumathaqri. “And a close one, too. Met in a hoverball virch a few cycles ago when we were both at the Hub. Se beat my ass like a pro, and we struck up a conversation while I was nursing my swollen pride. It turned out we had a lot of things besides sports in common, and, well, then se literally beat my ass.”

“I'm so sorry,” said Maranqura. “Is se doing okay?”

Sumathaqri nodded. “Last I heard. The worst that happened to sim was losing a leg to a girder; se got off lucky compared to most.”

“Yeah, I read the reports.” Maranqura shifted uncomfortably. “It’s not often we… y’know, lose people.”

“Thal’, Maran, just — don’t even go there,” said Sumathaqri. “I don’t need to be thinking about that right now.”

“Fuck, sorry. I’ll find someone else to vent to.”

“Thanks.”

“Yeah.” Maranqura sighed. “I think we got soft, y’know? We just got used to taking worlds from the Empire, and they were already tame, they were just a mess. Not like this, where we don’t know when the fucking geography is going to bite—” She frowned. “What’re the timetables for the evacuation with two skiffs out of the action?”

“Unchanged,” said Sumathaqri after a moment. “People might not be too comfortable on the lift out but we’ve still got enough skiffs to take them all in five trips.”

“Okay. Sorry, where was I? —Oh, I was going to say, think that mparisea of yours would want to join the dramatic reading party tonight?”

Sumathaqri shrugged. “I don’t think so; it’s not really ser kind of thing. We connected more over sports and physical stuff than, um, love of terrible fanfic.”

“Well, maybe we can find a few people it would cheer up.”

“I get the feeling you feel pretty bad for them.”

“Thal’, Sumath, of course I do. They just physically lost their ashtisa and… lost a few people they knew. They’ve been camped out in the snow for what, ten mroqa now? They didn’t sign up for that.”
